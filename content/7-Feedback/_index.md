### General Evaluation of the First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ) Program

Having completed the 12-week First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ) program, I would like to share my honest reflections and feedback regarding my learning and working experience:

**1. Learning & Working Environment**  
The FCAJ community environment is highly dynamic, open, and strongly encourages self-learning. Although it is a hybrid internship program combining online learning with offline events (Community Days and Study Tours), I always felt a strong sense of connection and a professional, enterprise-like working atmosphere.

**2. Mentorship & Admin Support**  
Mentors and Admins provided exceptional and timely support, especially when students encountered AWS infrastructure or account issues. The mentors focused on guiding our troubleshooting mindsets and problem-solving methodologies rather than just handing out answers, which greatly improved my debugging capabilities.

**3. Alignment Between Work & Academic Major**  
The curriculum—ranging from basic networking labs and IAM security configurations to serverless backend development (API Gateway, Lambda) and AI integration (Amazon Bedrock)—perfectly aligned with and supported my Software Engineering / Information Systems major. The final project "AI Resume Matching & Interview Preparation Platform" successfully turned theoretical 3-tier architecture concepts into a working application.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
The program equipped me with a solid cloud foundation and practical hands-on experience. More importantly, serving as the Team Leader allowed me to practice project planning, task management via Trello, hosting progress review meetings, and coordinating code reviews.

**5. Culture & Teamwork**  
The sharing and mutual support culture is the brightest highlight of FCAJ. During our group project development, team members willingly stayed up late to debug integration issues between the Front-end and Back-end. This strong collaborative spirit was a major driving force for me as a leader.

**6. Community Events**  
Events such as the **FCAJ Community Day** and the **Study Tour at AWS Office** (26th/36th floors of Bitexco Tower) were fantastic opportunities to network and learn directly from leading Solutions Architects at AWS Vietnam, helping bridge the gap between academia and industry.

---

### Response to Survey Questions

*   **What did you find most satisfying during the internship?**  
    The practical nature of the curriculum and the opportunity to own a complete AI-Cloud project from architectural design to deployment. Additionally, collaborating and learning alongside talented peers from other universities (such as HUTECH and Swinburne) provided highly valuable relationships and insights.
*   **What areas do you think the program should improve for future cohorts?**  
    The program could introduce basic guides or workshops on Terraform for Infrastructure as Code (IaC) in the mid-weeks. This would help final project teams manage and deploy their cloud resources consistently with fewer manual configuration errors.
*   **Would you recommend this program to your peers? Why?**  
    Definitely **YES**. It is an excellent launching pad for any student wanting to pursue Cloud, DevOps, or AI career paths. The program does not just teach how to use tools; it teaches how to solve real business problems—which is exactly what employers look for.

### Suggestions & Future Outlook
*   **Experience Improvement Suggestion:** The organizing committee could set up a centralized troubleshooting channel (e.g., Discord or a forum categorized by Lab weeks) so students can search for and resolve common lab errors more quickly.
*   **Future Involvement:** I would love to remain involved in the First Cloud Journey community as a sub-mentor or event support volunteer to help guide future cohorts and continue promoting cloud learning.
