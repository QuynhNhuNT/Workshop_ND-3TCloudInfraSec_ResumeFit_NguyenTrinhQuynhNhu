# Harvest Report: "FCAJ Community Day"

### Event Information
*   **Event Name:** FCAJ Community Day
*   **Event Date:** May 9, 2026
*   **Location:** 26th Floor, Bitexco Financial Tower, No. 02 Hai Trieu Street, Saigon Ward, Ho Chi Minh City
*   **Organizers:** AWS Study Group in collaboration with the FCAJ Community

### Event Objectives
*   **Program Goal:** To create an environment and opportunity for young individuals (especially 3rd and 4th-year students or fresh graduates) to confidently share knowledge, practice public speaking, build self-confidence, and receive career orientation.
*   **Key Messages:** Dopamine hacking for study motivation, Prompt Engineering optimization techniques, core mindset for IT professionals in the GenAI era, and standard software development workflows using AI (the BMAD methodology).
*   **Value for Attendees:** Help attendees overcome the fear of being replaced by AI, understand the paramount importance of foundational knowledge, learn how to leverage tools through structured workflows, and cultivate core career values like integrity and long-term thinking.

### Speaker List
*   **Speaker 1:** Khang Nguyen – Solution Architect at CloudKinetics
*   **Speaker 2:** Thinh Nguyen – DevOps Engineer | FCAJ Ambassador at AWS
*   **Speaker 3:** Long Hoang – FCAJ Ambassador at AWS
*   **Speaker 4:** Thao Nguyen Phuong – Cloud Engineer Intern at VIB | FCJ Ambassador at AWS

### Event Highlights
*   **Problem Overview:** Students and professionals often lack learning motivation and are easily distracted by social media due to a lack of "quick rewards". Additionally, in professional environments, over-relying on AI to write code directly without context generates bloated, disconnected code (due to AI hallucinations).
*   **Solutions Introduced:** 
    *   To study effectively: break goals down, set up short-term rewards, and build a streak (gamification) to trick the brain into releasing dopamine.
    *   To master AI: focus on "foundations", break tasks down into documentation templates (under the BMAD method) before allowing AI to generate code.
*   **Technologies, Services, & Tools:**
    *   **AWS Services:** CloudFront, S3 (storage), Cognito (auth), API Gateway, Lambda (serverless backend), Bedrock (AI models), DynamoDB (NoSQL Database), CloudWatch (monitoring).
    *   **AI Tools:** LLMs (Claude, Gemini), "Promptimizer" browser extension, BMAD framework.
*   **Demos & Case Studies:** 
    *   Presented a real-world Serverless and AI-powered architecture built on AWS.
    *   Demoed a browser extension named "Promptimizer" used to highlight text and instantly optimize prompts inside the browser.
*   **Key Takeaway:** AI acts as an **amplifier** of user capabilities: if your foundation is weak, it amplifies mistakes; if your foundation is solid, it boosts productivity exponentially.

### Key Learnings
*   **Mindset & Methodology:** Use AI to assist in brainstorming, but **never outsource your own understanding**. Always ask **"Why"** for every technical decision.
*   **Technical Knowledge:** Effective Prompt Engineering consists of: Role, Instruction, Context, Input, Output Format, and Constraints.
*   **Best Practices:** To prevent AI context bloat, feed it incremental inputs instead of a single giant prompt. In development, let AI play distinct roles (BM, Architect, Dev, Scrum Master) handling small, well-reviewed stories rather than handing over the entire project.
*   **Real-world Insights:** Recruiters do not just look for the correct final output; they evaluate the candidate's **thought process** and the reasoning behind their chosen solutions. Experience is not measured by time, but by the "richness of challenges" handled across different projects.

### Application to My Work
*   **Current Project Applicability:** Transform software development with AI by investing heavily in writing detailed technical documentation (VOD, System Architecture) first. Break projects down into Epics and Stories before having AI agents generate code and auto-review errors.
*   **Technologies to Explore:** Adopt AWS Lambda and Amazon Cognito to build serverless backends without managing physical servers or user password databases. Use the "Promptimizer" extension to improve prompt quality.
*   **Workflow Improvement Ideas:** Apply the **2-minute rule**: if a task takes less than 2 minutes to resolve, do it immediately without delay.

### Event Experience
*   **Speaker Insights:** Understood the 5 career pillars: Salary, Experience, Network, Knowledge, and Profile.
*   **Hands-on Experience:** Reviewing system design case studies and watching live Promptimizer demos provided highly practical and vivid insights.
*   **Networking:** The community strongly encourages members (including students) to speak up without fear of making mistakes or sharing things others might already know, emphasizing teamwork to go far.
*   **Most Memorable Aspect:** The concept of **Integrity**—proactively handling corner cases and delivering high-quality work regardless of whether it is inspected, building trust for the long haul.

### Key Takeaways
*   **Most Important Concept:** Master **foundational knowledge**; only when you understand the root of the problem can you accurately assess whether AI-generated outputs are correct or hallucinated.
*   **Real-world Experience:** Making mistakes is essential for growth, especially as a fresher. Companies are willing to pay for your mistakes as long as you maintain a learning mindset and improve.
*   **Future Development Plan:** Stay updated with new AWS announcements and tech trends to build structured knowledge. Strive to become a speaker who shares knowledge rather than a passive consumer of content. Build a resilient and persistent learning routine without expecting overnight results.
